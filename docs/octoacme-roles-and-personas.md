# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Test Engineers

### Role Summary
QA / Test Engineers help the team ship with confidence by defining how quality will be validated across development, release, and regression testing. They make acceptance expectations explicit and surface quality risks early.

### Responsibilities
- Define the test approach for new features, changes, and regression areas
- Validate acceptance criteria before work is marked complete
- Partner with developers on testability, coverage gaps, and defect triage
- Coordinate smoke tests, regression checks, and release verification
- Document defects, risks, and release-readiness concerns

### Goals
- Catch defects before release
- Improve confidence in delivery quality and readiness
- Reduce rework caused by unclear acceptance or incomplete testing

### Typical Communication
- Daily coordination with developers during implementation and bug triage
- Acceptance and readiness check-ins with Product and Project Managers
- Release verification updates in sprint demos, QA sign-off notes, or release channels

### Works Closely With
- Developers to improve testability, automation, and defect resolution
- Product Managers to confirm acceptance expectations and customer-facing quality
- Project Managers to flag schedule impacts, test dependencies, and release readiness

---

## Engineering Managers / Technical Leads

### Role Summary
Engineering Managers / Technical Leads provide technical direction and help the team make sound delivery trade-offs. They connect business goals to implementation choices, staffing needs, and technical risk management.

### Responsibilities
- Set technical direction and guide implementation decisions
- Support estimation, sequencing, and staffing discussions
- Identify architectural dependencies, constraints, and technical risks
- Coach developers through design reviews and execution challenges
- Help ensure non-functional requirements such as reliability, security, and maintainability are addressed

### Goals
- Keep delivery feasible, maintainable, and aligned with architecture goals
- Reduce technical surprises during execution and release
- Improve team effectiveness through clarity, coaching, and risk management

### Typical Communication
- Technical planning and design reviews with developers
- Trade-off discussions with Product Managers during scoping
- Delivery and dependency reviews with Project Managers

### Works Closely With
- Developers on design quality, execution blockers, and code health
- Product Managers on feasibility, scope trade-offs, and milestone risk
- Project Managers on sequencing, cross-team dependencies, and escalation paths

---

## Designers / UX Researchers

### Role Summary
Designers / UX Researchers clarify the user experience before and during delivery. They help the team understand user needs, validate assumptions, and translate product goals into clear flows, content, and usability decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Define user flows, wireframes, prototypes, or supporting design artifacts
- Validate problem statements and usability assumptions through research or feedback
- Identify edge cases, accessibility needs, and customer experience risks
- Partner with the team to keep implementation aligned with intended user outcomes
- Feed research insights back into prioritization and iteration decisions

### Goals
- Improve usability and clarity for end users
- Reduce rework caused by ambiguous flows or unmet user expectations
- Ensure solutions are grounded in customer needs and feedback

### Typical Communication
- Discovery sessions and problem-framing with Product Managers
- Design reviews and implementation clarifications with developers
- Planning coordination with Project Managers for research, reviews, and sign-off timing

### Works Closely With
- Product Managers on user problems, priorities, and success criteria
- Developers on implementation details, edge cases, and accessibility
- Project Managers on milestone timing for design reviews and feedback loops

---

## Stakeholders / Sponsors

### Role Summary
Stakeholders / Sponsors represent business, customer, or organizational interests that shape project priorities and success. They provide strategic direction, major approvals, and escalation support when delivery decisions affect outcomes beyond the immediate team.

### Responsibilities
- Clarify expected business outcomes and success measures
- Approve major scope, budget, timeline, or priority decisions when needed
- Remove organizational blockers and support escalations
- Review milestone progress and final outcomes against goals
- Provide context from adjacent functions such as sales, support, legal, or leadership

### Goals
- Ensure project work stays aligned with business priorities
- Help the team make timely decisions when trade-offs or escalations arise
- Improve adoption and organizational readiness for release

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews, decision meetings, and status updates
- Escalation discussions when risks affect delivery commitments
- Outcome reviews after launches or major releases

### Works Closely With
- Product Managers on priorities, success measures, and business trade-offs
- Project Managers on status, decisions, and escalation handling
- The broader delivery team during kickoff, milestone reviews, and release communications

---

## Support / Operations Representatives

### Role Summary
Support / Operations Representatives help the team deliver changes that are maintainable in production and supportable after release. They bring operational context into planning, rollout readiness, and post-release follow-through.

### Responsibilities
- Surface operational concerns, support trends, and customer-impact risks
- Define support-readiness needs such as runbooks, internal notes, or enablement
- Partner on rollout, monitoring, observability, and incident readiness
- Review changes for maintainability, recoverability, and handoff completeness
- Share post-release feedback to inform follow-up improvements

### Goals
- Reduce production surprises and support burden after launch
- Improve rollout readiness, observability, and incident response quality
- Ensure customer-facing teams are prepared for new changes

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with Project Managers and engineering
- Operational handoff notes, rollout plans, and incident follow-up
- Customer-impact and adoption feedback with Product Managers

### Works Closely With
- Project Managers on release plans, support readiness, and communication timing
- Developers and technical leads on observability, maintainability, and rollback considerations
- Product Managers on customer impact, known issues, and post-release follow-up
